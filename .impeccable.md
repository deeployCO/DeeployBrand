# Deeploy Brand — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Internal Deeploy team (designers, developers, marketers) and agency partners referencing brand assets during production. Context: professional work sessions at a desk, needing fast, authoritative answers on how to use the brand correctly. This is not a marketing surface — it's a working reference tool.

### Brand Personality
Results-driven, AI-forward, authoritative. Three words: **precise, forward, clean.**

Voice scores that matter for design decisions:
- Bold (7/10) — strong, confident visual choices; no timid sizing
- Innovative (8/10) — motion-first, digital-native, forward-leaning layouts
- Rational (not emotional) — lead with system logic, not feeling
- Serious (not playful) — B2B credibility; no whimsy

### Brand Tokens

**Colors:**
- Deploy Red: `#DF3E5B`
- Baby Blue: `#8BC9DB`
- Blue King (dark navy): `#172D3D`
- Background: White (dominant)
- Text: Near-black (tinted toward Blue King)

**Typography:**
- Primary: Avenir (full family — all weights available)
- No secondary typeface needed; Avenir's weight range handles full hierarchy
- Uppercase for headlines and standout labels; mixed case for body

**Photography style:** Professional, cinematic, speed/motion blur — people and objects in movement. No cheesy stock.

**Motion:** Animation is required in digital applications — the brand is motion-forward.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode. White-dominant. Generous negative space. The existing deeploy.ca site sets the right tone: clean white sections for content, dark cinematic photo overlays for hero moments.

Anti-references: anything saturated, overcrowded, or visually noisy. No gradient-heavy dashboards. No card-soup layouts. No "AI agency" purple/cyan clichés.

The design system itself should feel like a premium editorial document — confident, sparse, every element placed with intention. Think: high-end type foundry specimen or a Pentagram brand manual, not a Notion wiki.

### Design Principles
1. **White space is structure** — breathing room communicates confidence; tighter sections signal grouping, not compression
2. **Color is an accent, not a wash** — Deploy Red and Baby Blue appear sparingly; the system earns its restraint
3. **Type hierarchy over decoration** — Avenir's weight range (Light → Black) is the primary visual tool; no supplementary decoration needed
4. **Motion implies speed** — animation is brand-native; when motion is used, it should feel like acceleration, not flair
5. **Precision over personality** — every rule in the system should feel deliberate and defensible, not stylistic
